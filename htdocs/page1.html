Tips & Tricks

Adjusting to our current situation might be hard, so a few tips and tricks could definitely go a long way. These tips are partly from personal experience and partly from research online.

Tip #1

Socialize. Talk with your friends online through chat or video calls, they’re going through the same situation you are and can probably help you cope. And with all the stress of remote-learning, socialization can help reduce stress levels.

Tip #2

Manage your time. Create a schedule, set timers, and take note of everything you have to do. Cramming overworks the brain and can cause stress, fatigue, confusion, and such. When making a schedule, make sure you have cushion time between all your blocks of activities.

Tip #3

Take breaks. A 2012 study by Mary Helen Immordino-Yang found that the “default mode” of our brain, its state whenever resting, is very important for understanding the world. Breaks also help in stress management.

Tip #4

Eliminate Distractions. Distractions can make learning less efficient and useful and take the focus away from the current task at hand, and this can lead to problems with time management. According to Russ Poldrack, a Stanford neuroscientist, information may be sent to the wrong parts of the brain if you multitask.

Tip #5

Treat Remote-Learning like Regular Classes. Just because deadlines aren’t as strict you can keep putting things off until the last minute. It’s also easier to stay focused on your tasks if your brain associates your classes with your regular routine.
